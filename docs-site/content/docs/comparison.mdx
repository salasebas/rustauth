Here are high-level reasons to choose RustAuth over other auth libraries and services.

### vs Other Auth Libraries

No other Rust auth library ships the same breadth of plugins, enterprise features, and storage adapters in one server-first toolkit. Most alternatives cover a slice—sessions, OAuth, or passkeys—but not the full surface RustAuth targets out of the box.

* **Plugins and features in one place** - 2FA, passkeys, organizations, API keys, magic links, OAuth/OIDC provider, SCIM, SAML, rate limiting, and more without stitching together half a dozen libraries
* **Server-first Rust** - Auth primitives, HTTP routes, and adapters live on your Rust server, not in a separate SaaS or JS-only runtime
* **Plugin system** - Extend behavior with official plugins instead of forking or maintaining one-off integrations
* **Storage flexibility** - SQLx, tokio-postgres, deadpool, Redis, and in-memory adapters behind a shared contract
* **Full control** - Customize flows, options, and deployment while keeping users and sessions in your stack

### vs Managed Auth Services

* **Keep your data** - Users stay in your database, not a third-party service
* **No per-user costs** - Scale without worrying about auth billing
* **Single source of truth** - All user data in one place
* **Self-host anywhere** - Deploy on your own infrastructure with full control

### vs Rolling Your Own

* **Security handled** - Battle-tested auth flows and security practices
* **Focus on your product** - Spend time on features that matter to your business
* **Plugin extensibility** - Add custom features without starting from scratch
* **Production ready** - Works embedded in your app or as a dedicated auth server
